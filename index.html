The toughest man alive

Here's a time line of David Goggin's life:

  • 1975 - Born in Buffalo, New York
  • 1994-1998 - Served his time in United States Air Force Tactical Air Control Party (TACP)
  • 1999 - Weighing almost 300 pounds, Goggins was told he was too heavy to make it through SEAL training. In less than three months, he returned weighing 190 pounds
  • 2001 - After three attempts, Goggins succeeded in graduating from the BUD/S training with Class 235 and was assigned to SEAL Team FIVE
  • 2004 - Goggins graduated from Army Ranger School with the distinction of enlisted "Top Honor Man"
  • 2005 - Several of his friends die in Afghanistan in a helicopter crash during Operation Red Wings. Goggins begins long-distance running with the aim of raising money for the Special Operations Warrior Foundation
  • 2008 - He was named a "Hero of Running" by Runner's World
  • 2012 - Goggins went on the Today Show to attempt to beat the world record for the most pull-ups done in 24 hours. He completed 2,588 pullups by the end of the event. Despite not beating the record, Goggins raised more than $20,000 for the Special Operations Warrior Foundation
  • 2013 - At Crossfit Brentwood Hills, Goggins broke the world record for the most pull-ups done in 24 hours, completing 4,030 pull-ups in 17 hours
  • 2016 - Goggins won the Infinitus 88k in 12 hours, almost twenty minutes ahead of the next finisher
  • 2018 - Goggins releases his self-help memoir, Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds
